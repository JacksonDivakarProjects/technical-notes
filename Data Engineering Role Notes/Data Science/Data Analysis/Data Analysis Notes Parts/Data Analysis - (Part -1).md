# The Business Data Analysis Playbook
*A Non-Technical Guide to Finding What Matters in Your Data*

## Part 1: The Foundation - Understanding Your Business Through Data

### The 5 Universal Business Questions Every Dataset Can Answer

**1. The Performance Question:** "Are we growing or declining?"
**2. The Customer Question:** "Who buys from us and why?"
**3. The Product Question:** "What should we sell more/less of?"
**4. The Timing Question:** "When should we push harder?"
**5. The Location Question:** "Where should we focus our efforts?"

---

## Part 2: The Analysis Framework - What to Look For

### Section A: Time-Based Analysis (The "When")

**Trend Analysis - What to Look For:**
- Upward trends → What's working? Do more of it
- Downward trends → Early warning system - investigate immediately
- Flat lines → Stagnation risk - needs innovation
- Sudden spikes/drops → What caused this? Can we replicate/avoid?

**Seasonality Patterns - What to Look For:**
- Monthly patterns → Plan inventory and staffing
- Weekly patterns → Schedule promotions and staffing
- Holiday effects → Prepare marketing campaigns in advance
- Weather-related patterns → Stock appropriate products

**Key Questions for Time Analysis:**
- "Which month was our best ever? Why?"
- "Is there a day of week that consistently underperforms?"
- "How does this quarter compare to same quarter last year?"
- "Are we growing faster or slower than last year?"

### Section B: Customer Analysis (The "Who")

**Customer Segmentation - The Practical Approach:**

**1. The VIP Identification:**
- Find customers in top 10% by spending
- Look for patterns: What do they buy? When? How often?
- **Action:** Create exclusive offers for this group

**2. The Loyal Regulars:**
- Frequent buyers but medium spending
- **Action:** Reward loyalty with points programs

**3. The At-Risk Customers:**
- Used to buy frequently but haven't recently
- **Action:** Win-back campaigns with special offers

**4. The One-Timers:**
- Bought once, never returned
- **Action:** Understand why they didn't come back

**Demographic Insights - What Matters:**
- Age groups that spend the most
- Geographic locations with highest value customers
- Common characteristics of your best customers

### Section C: Product Analysis (The "What")

**Performance Ranking - Beyond Just Sales:**

**The Profitability Matrix:**
```
High Sales + High Profit = STARS (Invest heavily)
High Sales + Low Profit = WORKHORSES (Optimize costs)
Low Sales + High Profit = POTENTIAL STARS (Market better)
Low Sales + Low Profit = DOGS (Consider dropping)
```

**Category Health Check:**
- Growing categories → Invest more resources
- Declining categories → Understand why, consider pivoting
- Seasonal categories → Time your inventory and marketing
- Complementary categories → Bundle products together

**Inventory Intelligence:**
- Fast-moving items → Never run out
- Slow-moving items → Don't over-order
- Dead stock → Clearance or bundle offers

### Section D: Geographic Analysis (The "Where")

**Regional Performance Map:**

**High-Performing Regions:**
- What's working there? Can we replicate it?
- Are we at saturation point?
- **Action:** Protect and maintain

**Under-Performing Regions:**
- Why are sales low? Competition? Awareness?
- **Action:** Investigate and test new approaches

**Emerging Regions:**
- Showing recent growth
- **Action:** Fuel the growth with additional investment

**Shipping & Cost Analysis:**
- Regions with high shipping costs vs revenue
- Delivery time performance by region
- **Action:** Optimize logistics or adjust pricing

## Part 3: From Insight to Action - The Decision Framework

### How to Interpret Common Patterns:

**Pattern:** "Sales spike every December"
**Interpretation:** Strong seasonal business
**Actions:**
- Build inventory in October/November
- Hire temporary staff in advance
- Run pre-holiday marketing campaigns
- Plan for post-holiday slowdown

**Pattern:** "20% of customers generate 80% of revenue"
**Interpretation:** Heavy reliance on VIP customers
**Actions:**
- Create VIP loyalty program
- Assign account managers for top customers
- Develop exclusive products/services for this group
- Work on diversifying customer base

**Pattern:** "Product A sells well but has low profit margins"
**Interpretation:** Loss leader or pricing issue
**Actions:**
- Consider price increase for loyal customers
- Bundle with high-margin products
- Reduce costs through supplier negotiation
- Use as acquisition tool for new customers

**Pattern:** "Online sales growing but in-store declining"
**Interpretation:** Channel shift happening
**Actions:**
- Invest in online experience
- Make stores more experiential
- Implement click-and-collect services
- Train staff for new customer service model

### The Red Flag Checklist:

**Immediate Investigation Needed When You See:**
- Sudden 20%+ drop in sales (any time period)
- Best-selling product suddenly stops selling
- Key customer stops ordering
- Regional performance collapses
- Profit margins shrinking consistently

## Part 4: Industry-Specific Cheat Sheets

### Retail/E-commerce Focus:

**Critical Metrics:**
- Conversion rate (website visitors to buyers)
- Average order value
- Cart abandonment rate
- Customer acquisition cost
- Return rate by product

**Key Questions:**
- "Which marketing channel brings the best customers?"
- "What products are often bought together?"
- "What's our return rate and why?"
- "How does mobile vs desktop shopping differ?"

### SaaS/Software Business:

**Critical Metrics:**
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- Customer churn rate
- Lifetime value (LTV)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Feature usage patterns

**Key Questions:**
- "Which features do paying customers use most?"
- "Why do customers cancel?"
- "What's the trial-to-paid conversion rate?"
- "Which marketing channel has best LTV:CAC ratio?"

### Service Business (Consulting, Agencies):

**Critical Metrics:**
- Utilization rate (billable hours)
- Client retention rate
- Project profitability
- Client lifetime value
- Service delivery costs

**Key Questions:**
- "Which services are most profitable?"
- "Why do clients leave?"
- "What's the cost of delivering each service?"
- "Which client type is most valuable?"

### Restaurant/Food Business:

**Critical Metrics:**
- Table turnover rate
- Average spend per customer
- Popular menu items
- Peak hours analysis
- Food cost percentages

**Key Questions:**
- "What are our peak days/hours?"
- "Which menu items have best margins?"
- "How does weather affect sales?"
- "What's the average party size and spend?"

## Part 5: The Execution Framework - Making It Happen

### Weekly Health Check (30 minutes):
1. Sales vs last week
2. Top 5 products performance
3. New customer acquisition
4. Any red flags from above

### Monthly Deep Dive (2 hours):
1. Full trend analysis
2. Customer segment performance
3. Product category review
4. Regional performance
5. Profitability analysis

### Quarterly Strategy Session (Half day):
1. Review all insights from past quarter
2. Identify 3 key opportunities
3. Identify 3 key risks
4. Create action plan for next quarter

### The One-Page Dashboard Concept:

**What every manager should see at a glance:**
- Current month sales vs target
- Growth rate vs same period last year
- Top performing product/category
- Customer acquisition trend
- Key metric that matters most for your business
