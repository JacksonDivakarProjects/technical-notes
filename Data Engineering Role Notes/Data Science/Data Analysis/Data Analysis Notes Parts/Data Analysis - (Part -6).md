# Part 6: Real-World Business Scenarios - From Data to Dollars

## Actual Business Problems and How to Solve Them With Data

### Scenario 1: The "Mystery" Sales Drop

**The Problem:**
"Last month, sales dropped 15% for no obvious reason. No one knows why."

**The Data Investigation:**
```
Step 1: Segment the drop
- Which products declined most?
- Which customer segments dropped off?
- Which regions underperformed?
- Which sales channels declined?

Step 2: Check timing patterns
- When exactly did the drop start?
- Was it gradual or sudden?
- Any correlation with external events?

Step 3: Investigate causes
- Pricing changes?
- Competitor activity?
- Website/operational issues?
- Customer feedback trends?
```

**Real Case Example:**
*A restaurant saw 20% revenue drop. Analysis showed:*
- Decline started exactly 3 weeks ago
- Only affected dinner service, not lunch
- Mostly impacted groups of 4+ people
- **Root cause:** New parking restrictions made evening parking difficult for groups
- **Solution:** Validated parking and promoted it - recovered sales in 2 weeks

### Scenario 2: The "We're Busy But Broke" Problem

**The Problem:**
"We're working constantly, revenue is growing, but profits are shrinking."

**The Profitability Investigation:**
```
Step 1: Find what's actually profitable
- Calculate profit margin by product/service
- Identify hidden costs by customer type
- Analyze time spent vs revenue earned

Step 2: Identify cost creep
- Which costs grew faster than revenue?
- Where are we over-servicing?
- What efficiencies have we lost?

Step 3: Customer profitability analysis
- Rank customers by actual profit (not revenue)
- Identify "vampire" customers draining resources
- Find your truly profitable segments
```

**Real Case Example:**
*A marketing agency was "successful" but losing money. Analysis revealed:*
- 80% of profit came from 20% of clients
- Small clients demanded same attention as large ones
- Certain services had negative margins after accounting for time
- **Solution:** Fired bottom 10% of clients, raised prices 30% for mid-tier, focused on profitable services - doubled profits in 60 days

### Scenario 3: The Inventory Headache

**The Problem:**
"Either we're out of stock on popular items or sitting on dead inventory."

**The Inventory Optimization:**
```
Step 1: ABC Analysis
- A items: Top 20% by revenue (never run out)
- B items: Middle 30% (optimize carefully)  
- C items: Bottom 50% (minimize investment)

Step 2: Sales velocity analysis
- Fast movers: <30 days turnover
- Medium movers: 30-90 days turnover  
- Slow movers: 90+ days turnover
- Dead stock: No sales in 6+ months

Step 3: Seasonal pattern recognition
- When do items peak/decline?
- Lead time considerations
- Minimum order quantities vs storage costs
```

**Real Case Example:**
*A retail store had constant stock issues. Analysis showed:*
- 15% of products generated 60% of revenue
- Popular items frequently out of stock
- 40% of inventory hadn't sold in 6 months
- **Solution:** Created "never out" list for top products, clearance sale for dead stock, implemented simple weekly stock counts - reduced stockouts by 80% and freed up 30% of storage space

## The "What Should We Do Next?" Framework

### When You Have Multiple Opportunities:

**Opportunity Scoring Matrix:**
Rate each opportunity 1-5 on:
- Potential Revenue Impact
- Ease of Implementation  
- Speed to Results
- Strategic Importance

**Prioritization Formula:**
```
(Revenue Impact × Strategic Importance) ÷ (Implementation Difficulty × Time to Results)
```

**Example: Choosing Between Projects:**
```
Project A: New product line
- Revenue: 5/5, Strategic: 4/5, Difficulty: 4/5, Time: 3/5
Score: (5×4) ÷ (4×3) = 1.67

Project B: Price optimization  
- Revenue: 3/5, Strategic: 3/5, Difficulty: 2/5, Time: 2/5
Score: (3×3) ÷ (2×2) = 2.25

Result: Do Project B first - better return on effort
```

## Crisis Response Playbook

### When Things Go Wrong - Data-Driven Damage Control

**30-Day Recovery Plan Template:**

**Week 1: Stabilize**
- Identify the exact scope of the problem
- Implement immediate cash preservation
- Communicate with key stakeholders
- Stop all non-essential spending

**Week 2: Diagnose**
- Root cause analysis using Part 2 framework
- Customer retention focus
- Cost structure emergency review
- Quick win identification

**Week 3: Execute**
- Implement identified quick wins
- Launch retention campaigns
- Renegotiate key costs
- Measure early results daily

**Week 4: Plan**
- Based on recovery trajectory, plan next steps
- Adjust business model if needed
- Set new realistic targets
- Implement ongoing monitoring

## Common Analysis Mistakes to Avoid

### Lessons from Failed Data Projects:

**Mistake 1: Analysis Paralysis**
- **Symptom:** Endless reports, no decisions
- **Solution:** Time-box analysis, focus on actionable insights

**Mistake 2: Vanity Metrics**
- **Symptom:** Tracking numbers that don't drive decisions
- **Solution:** Connect every metric to a business action

**Mistake 3: Data Quality Obsession**
- **Symptom:** Waiting for perfect data before analyzing
- **Solution:** Work with what you have, improve gradually

**Mistake 4: Ignoring Business Context**
- **Symptom:** Data says one thing, reality says another
- **Solution:** Combine data with frontline experience

**Mistake 5: One-Size-Fits-All Approach**
- **Symptom:** Applying generic benchmarks without context
- **Solution:** Understand your unique business model
