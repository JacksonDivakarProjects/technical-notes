## 🧠 What “Put” Means Generally

At its core, **“put”** means _to place something somewhere._  
But when combined with **prepositions/adverbs**, its meaning changes dramatically — often expressing actions, effort, delay, or confrontation.

---

## ⚙️ Core Phrasal Verbs with “Put” (with meaning, example, and context)

### 1. **Put off** — _delay or postpone_

- **Meaning:** To delay something to a later time.
    
- **Example:** _Let’s not put off the meeting again; deadlines are tight._
    
- **Synonyms:** postpone, defer
    
- **Antonyms:** advance, expedite
    
- **Usage Tip:** In business — _“Let’s not put off the decision any longer.”_
    

---

### 2. **Put up with** — _tolerate or endure_

- **Meaning:** To accept something unpleasant without complaining.
    
- **Example:** _I can’t put up with his excuses anymore._
    
- **Synonyms:** endure, bear, tolerate
    
- **Antonyms:** reject, resist
    
- **Professional Context:** _We shouldn’t put up with inefficiency in the workflow._
    

---

### 3. **Put out** — _disturb / extinguish / release_

- **Meanings:**
    
    - (1) **Extinguish:** _They put out the fire quickly._
        
    - (2) **Annoy/Disturb:** _I hope I didn’t put you out by calling so late._
        
    - (3) **Release something:** _The company put out a press release._
        
- **Synonyms:** extinguish, inconvenience, issue
    
- **Antonyms:** ignite, comfort, withhold
    

---

### 4. **Put down** — _insult / suppress / record_

- **Meanings:**
    
    - (1) **Insult:** _He’s always putting his colleagues down in meetings._
        
    - (2) **Write or record:** _Please put down your observations in the report._
        
    - (3) **Suppress:** _The police put down the protest peacefully._
        
- **Synonyms:** humiliate, note, suppress
    
- **Antonyms:** praise, uplift, encourage
    

---

### 5. **Put aside** — _save / ignore temporarily_

- **Meaning:**
    
    - (1) **Save money/time:** _I put aside 10% of my income each month._
        
    - (2) **Ignore differences:** _Let’s put aside our opinions and focus on results._
        
- **Synonyms:** save, reserve, disregard
    
- **Antonyms:** spend, consider
    

---

### 6. **Put together** — _assemble or organize_

- **Meaning:** To build, prepare, or organize something.
    
- **Example:** _She put together a brilliant proposal overnight._
    
- **Synonyms:** assemble, compile, create
    
- **Antonyms:** disassemble, dismantle
    
- **Work Usage:** _Let’s put together a business case for this idea._
    

---

### 7. **Put across** — _communicate clearly_

- **Meaning:** To express an idea effectively.
    
- **Example:** _You put your ideas across very clearly in the presentation._
    
- **Synonyms:** convey, articulate, express
    
- **Antonyms:** confuse, obscure
    

---

### 8. **Put through** — _connect / make experience_

- **Meanings:**
    
    - (1) **Connect on phone:** _I’ll put you through to HR._
        
    - (2) **Make experience:** _They put him through a tough training program._
        
- **Synonyms:** transfer, subject, connect
    
- **Antonyms:** disconnect, protect
    

---

### 9. **Put up** — _provide / erect / stay_

- **Meanings:**
    
    - (1) **Provide accommodation:** _They put me up for the night._
        
    - (2) **Erect:** _We’re putting up a new office building._
        
    - (3) **Offer:** _They put up a strong fight._
        
- **Synonyms:** accommodate, build, offer
    
- **Antonyms:** demolish, take down
    

---

### 10. **Put forward** — _suggest or propose_

- **Meaning:** To present an idea or plan for consideration.
    
- **Example:** _He put forward a new strategy during the meeting._
    
- **Synonyms:** propose, suggest, submit
    
- **Antonyms:** withdraw, dismiss
    

---

### 11. **Put in** — _invest or contribute_

- **Meaning:**
    
    - (1) **Contribute effort/time:** _She’s put in a lot of hours this week._
        
    - (2) **Submit formally:** _He put in his resignation._
        
- **Synonyms:** invest, contribute, submit
    
- **Antonyms:** withdraw, take out
    

---

### 12. **Put on** — _wear / perform / pretend_

- **Meanings:**
    
    - (1) **Wear:** _He put on his jacket._
        
    - (2) **Organize:** _They’re putting on a charity event._
        
    - (3) **Pretend:** _Don’t put on that accent—it’s not natural._
        
- **Synonyms:** wear, stage, fake
    
- **Antonyms:** take off, remove
    

---

### 13. **Put back** — _return or delay_

- **Meaning:**
    
    - (1) **Return to original place:** _Put the files back in the cabinet._
        
    - (2) **Delay:** _The meeting’s been put back to 4 PM._
        
- **Synonyms:** replace, postpone
    
- **Antonyms:** take out, advance
    

---

### 14. **Put up for** — _offer for sale or nomination_

- **Meaning:** To make something available for sale or a person for election.
    
- **Example:** _They put the old warehouse up for sale._
    
- **Synonyms:** offer, propose
    
- **Antonyms:** keep, withhold
    

---

### 15. **Put upon** — _take advantage of someone_

- **Meaning:** To be treated unfairly or burdened.
    
- **Example:** _He felt put upon by his coworkers._
    
- **Synonyms:** exploited, overworked
    
- **Antonyms:** respected, supported
    

---

## 💼 Bonus Tip for Professional English

In business communication, **“put forward,” “put together,” and “put across”** are the most useful.  
They make you sound polished and collaborative:

- _“I’d like to put forward a few alternatives.”_
    
- _“Let’s put together a quick demo.”_
    
- _“You’ve put that across beautifully.”_
