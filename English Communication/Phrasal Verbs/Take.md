Here’s a **comprehensive guide to phrasal verbs with “TAKE”** — a must-know for sounding fluent and professional in English.

---

## 🔹 1. TAKE AFTER (someone)

**Meaning:** Resemble a family member in appearance or behavior.  
**Example:** _Jack takes after his father in his calm attitude._  
**Synonyms:** resemble, mirror, reflect  
**Antonyms:** differ from, contrast with

---

## 🔹 2. TAKE APART

**Meaning:** Disassemble something into pieces OR criticize severely.  
**Example 1 (literal):** _The mechanic took apart the engine to check the fault._  
**Example 2 (figurative):** _The reviewer took apart the movie for its weak plot._  
**Synonyms:** dismantle, deconstruct, tear down  
**Antonyms:** assemble, build, construct

---

## 🔹 3. TAKE BACK

**Meaning:**

1. Admit something said was wrong.
    
2. Return an item.  
    **Example 1:** _I take back what I said about you._  
    **Example 2:** _The store doesn’t take back used items._  
    **Synonyms:** retract, withdraw, return  
    **Antonyms:** confirm, keep
    

---

## 🔹 4. TAKE DOWN

**Meaning:**

1. Write or record information.
    
2. Remove something from a high place.
    
3. Defeat or humble someone.  
    **Example:** _The police took down his statement._  
    **Synonyms:** jot down, dismantle, remove, defeat  
    **Antonyms:** put up, build, install
    

---

## 🔹 5. TAKE IN

**Meaning:**

1. Understand or absorb information.
    
2. Allow someone to stay in your home.
    
3. Deceive someone.  
    **Example 1:** _It’s hard to take in all this information at once._  
    **Example 2:** _They took in an orphaned child._  
    **Example 3:** _Don’t be taken in by his lies._  
    **Synonyms:** comprehend, shelter, deceive  
    **Antonyms:** ignore, exclude, reveal
    

---

## 🔹 6. TAKE OFF

**Meaning:**

1. (Plane) Leave the ground.
    
2. Become successful suddenly.
    
3. Remove clothing.
    
4. Imitate someone humorously.  
    **Example 1:** _The flight took off on time._  
    **Example 2:** _Her career took off after that commercial._  
    **Synonyms:** ascend, succeed, remove, mimic  
    **Antonyms:** land, fail, put on
    

---

## 🔹 7. TAKE ON

**Meaning:**

1. Accept responsibility or a challenge.
    
2. Employ someone.
    
3. Compete against someone.  
    **Example 1:** _She took on a new project._  
    **Example 2:** _The company is taking on new interns._  
    **Example 3:** _He took on the champion and won._  
    **Synonyms:** accept, hire, confront  
    **Antonyms:** refuse, fire, surrender
    

---

## 🔹 8. TAKE OUT

**Meaning:**

1. Remove or extract something.
    
2. Invite someone (e.g., for dinner).
    
3. Obtain (insurance, loan, etc.).  
    **Example 1:** _He took out his wallet._  
    **Example 2:** _I’ll take you out for dinner tonight._  
    **Example 3:** _They took out life insurance._  
    **Synonyms:** extract, escort, acquire  
    **Antonyms:** insert, decline
    

---

## 🔹 9. TAKE OVER

**Meaning:**

1. Assume control or responsibility.
    
2. Replace someone in a job or position.  
    **Example:** _The new manager took over last week._  
    **Synonyms:** assume, control, dominate  
    **Antonyms:** relinquish, surrender, hand over
    

---

## 🔹 10. TAKE UP

**Meaning:**

1. Start a hobby or activity.
    
2. Occupy time, space, or attention.
    
3. Discuss or deal with an issue.  
    **Example 1:** _He took up painting during lockdown._  
    **Example 2:** _This project takes up most of my time._  
    **Example 3:** _We’ll take this up in the next meeting._  
    **Synonyms:** begin, occupy, address  
    **Antonyms:** quit, free, ignore
    

---

## 🔹 11. TAKE TO

**Meaning:**

1. Develop a liking for something/someone.
    
2. Begin a new habit.  
    **Example:** _She took to yoga immediately._  
    **Synonyms:** warm up to, adapt to, start  
    **Antonyms:** dislike, avoid
    

---

## 🔹 12. TAKE AWAY

**Meaning:**

1. Remove something.
    
2. Learn or gain from an experience.
    
3. (UK) Food bought to eat elsewhere.  
    **Example 1:** _Take away these dishes, please._  
    **Example 2:** _What I took away from that seminar was clarity._  
    **Synonyms:** remove, learn, takeaway (noun)  
    **Antonyms:** add, bring in
    

---

## 🔹 13. TAKE THROUGH

**Meaning:**  
Explain or guide someone step-by-step.  
**Example:** _Let me take you through the process._  
**Synonyms:** explain, guide, walk through  
**Antonyms:** confuse, skip

---

## 🔹 14. TAKE UPON (oneself)

**Meaning:**  
Assume responsibility or authority without being asked.  
**Example:** _He took it upon himself to fix the issue._  
**Synonyms:** assume, undertake  
**Antonyms:** delegate, avoid

---

## 🔹 15. TAKE BACK (Memory context)

**Meaning:**  
Remind someone of the past.  
**Example:** _That song takes me back to my college days._  
**Synonyms:** remind, recall  
**Antonyms:** forget, overlook

---

### ⚙️ Professional Usage Tip:

When speaking in meetings or presentations:

- Instead of saying _“start doing”_, say **“take up”**.
    
- Instead of _“understand”_, say **“take in”**.
    
- Instead of _“handle”_, say **“take on”**.
    
- Instead of _“replace”_, say **“take over”**.
