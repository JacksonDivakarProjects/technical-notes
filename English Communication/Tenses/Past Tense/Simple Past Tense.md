## **Simple Past Tense – Comprehensive Guide**

### **1️⃣ Definition**

The **Simple Past Tense** expresses:

- Actions that **happened and finished in the past**.
    
- Events at a **specific time in the past** (often mentioned or implied).
    
- Habits or repeated actions **in the past**.
    

It’s commonly used in **storytelling, reporting past events, and professional updates**.

---

### **2️⃣ Structure**

**A. Affirmative Sentences**

|Subject|Verb Form (Past)|Example|
|---|---|---|
|I / You / We / They / He / She / It|Regular: verb + -ed|I finished the report yesterday.|
||Irregular: change form|She went to the office yesterday.|

**Notes:**

- Regular verbs → add **-ed** → work → worked
    
- Irregular verbs → must memorize → go → went, see → saw, eat → ate
    

---

**B. Negative Sentences**

|Subject|Auxiliary + Not|Base Verb|Example|
|---|---|---|---|
|I / You / We / They / He / She / It|did not (didn’t)|base verb|I didn’t finish the report yesterday.|

**Important:** In negative forms, **use base verb** (no -ed or irregular past).

---

**C. Interrogative Sentences**

|Structure|Example|
|---|---|
|Did + subject + base verb?|Did you finish the report yesterday?|

**Tip:** Use **did** for all subjects; the main verb stays in base form.

---

### **3️⃣ Key Uses with Examples**

1. **Completed actions in the past**
    
    - I watched a movie last night.
        
    - They visited the client last week.
        
2. **Specific past time**
    
    - She called me at 9 AM.
        
    - We met him yesterday.
        
3. **Past habits or repeated actions**
    
    - He played football every weekend.
        
    - I often studied late at night.
        
4. **Narration / storytelling**
    
    - I woke up early, had breakfast, and went to work.
        

---

### **4️⃣ Common Mistakes**

|Mistake|Correct Form|Explanation|
|---|---|---|
|She go to the office yesterday.|She went to the office yesterday.|Irregular verb needed.|
|I didn’t went to the meeting.|I didn’t go to the meeting.|After **did**, use base verb.|
|Did he went to the client?|Did he go to the client?|After **did**, use base verb.|
|I was finished the report yesterday.|I finished the report yesterday.|Avoid mixing simple past with past participle unnecessarily.|

---

### **5️⃣ Tips for Mastery**

- Use **past form of the verb** for completed actions.
    
- Use **did/didn’t** for negative and interrogative forms.
    
- Always mention **time reference** if possible (yesterday, last week, in 2019).
    
- Memorize **common irregular verbs** for accurate usage.
