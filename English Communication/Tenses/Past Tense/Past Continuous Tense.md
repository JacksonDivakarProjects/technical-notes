## **Past Continuous Tense – Comprehensive Guide**

### **1️⃣ Definition**

The **Past Continuous Tense** expresses:

- Actions that were **in progress at a specific time in the past**.
    
- **Parallel actions** happening at the same time in the past.
    
- **Interrupted actions** in the past.
    

It’s commonly used in **storytelling, describing past situations, and setting context in professional reporting**.

---

### **2️⃣ Structure**

**A. Affirmative Sentences**

|Subject|Auxiliary Verb|Main Verb + ing|Example|
|---|---|---|---|
|I / He / She / It|was|working|I was working on the report at 9 AM.|
|You / We / They|were|working|They were discussing the project yesterday.|

---

**B. Negative Sentences**

|Subject|Auxiliary + Not|Main Verb + ing|Example|
|---|---|---|---|
|I / He / She / It|was not (wasn’t)|working|I wasn’t working at 9 AM.|
|You / We / They|were not (weren’t)|working|They weren’t discussing the project.|

---

**C. Interrogative Sentences**

|Structure|Example|
|---|---|
|Was + subject + verb-ing?|Was she working on the report at 9 AM?|
|Were + subject + verb-ing?|Were they discussing the project yesterday?|

---

### **3️⃣ Key Uses with Examples**

1. **Action in progress at a specific past time**
    
    - I was reading a book at 8 PM.
        
    - He was driving to the office when it started raining.
        
2. **Parallel actions (two actions happening simultaneously)**
    
    - I was cooking while he was watching TV.
        
    - She was studying while her brother was playing outside.
        
3. **Interrupted past action**
    
    - I was taking a shower when the phone rang.
        
    - They were having a meeting when the fire alarm went off.
        
4. **Background information for storytelling**
    
    - It was raining, and people were running to take shelter.
        
    - The manager was explaining the project while the team was taking notes.
        

---

### **4️⃣ Common Mistakes**

|Mistake|Correct Form|Explanation|
|---|---|---|
|I working at 9 AM yesterday.|I was working at 9 AM yesterday.|Missing auxiliary **was**.|
|She were studying when I called.|She was studying when I called.|Subject-verb agreement: **she → was**.|
|Were you worked on the report?|Were you working on the report?|Use **verb-ing**, not past simple.|
|He wasn’t work when I arrived.|He wasn’t working when I arrived.|Use **verb-ing** for past continuous.|

---

### **5️⃣ Tips for Mastery**

- Always use **was/were + verb-ing**.
    
- Use **was** for singular subjects (I, he, she, it) and **were** for plural subjects (you, we, they).
    
- Combine with **when** for interrupted actions.
    
- Use past continuous to **set background scenes or context**.
