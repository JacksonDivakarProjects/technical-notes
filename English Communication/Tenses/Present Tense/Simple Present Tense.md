## **Simple Present Tense – Comprehensive Guide**

### **1️⃣ Definition**

The **Simple Present Tense** expresses:

- General truths or facts.
    
- Habits or routines.
    
- Scheduled events (like timetables).
    
- Instructions or directions.
    
- Feelings, opinions, or states.
    

It is one of the most frequently used tenses in English for **daily communication and professional contexts**.

---

### **2️⃣ Structure**

**A. Affirmative Sentences**

|Subject|Verb Form|Example|
|---|---|---|
|I / You / We / They|base verb|I play football every day.|
|He / She / It|base verb + **-s/-es**|She plays football every day.|

**Notes on -s/-es:**

- Add **-s** to most verbs: play → plays
    
- Add **-es** for verbs ending in: -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -o → watch → watches
    
- For verbs ending with consonant + y, change y → i + es → study → studies
    

---

**B. Negative Sentences**

|Subject|Auxiliary + Not|Base Verb|Example|
|---|---|---|---|
|I / You / We / They|do not (don’t)|base verb|I don’t like coffee.|
|He / She / It|does not (doesn’t)|base verb|She doesn’t like coffee.|

**Important:** In negative forms, **we never add -s/-es to the main verb**.

---

**C. Interrogative Sentences**

|Structure|Example|
|---|---|
|Do + subject + base verb?|Do you play football?|
|Does + subject + base verb?|Does he play football?|

**Tip:** Use **does** for third-person singular, **do** for others.

---

### **3️⃣ Key Uses with Examples**

1. **General Truths / Facts**
    
    - The sun rises in the east.
        
    - Water boils at 100°C.
        
2. **Habit / Routine**
    
    - I wake up at 6 AM every day.
        
    - She goes to the gym twice a week.
        
3. **Scheduled Events / Timetables**
    
    - The train leaves at 9 PM tonight.
        
    - Classes start at 8 AM.
        
4. **Instructions / Directions**
    
    - First, boil the water.
        
    - Turn right at the traffic signal.
        
5. **Feelings, Opinions, or States**
    
    - I like chocolate.
        
    - He loves reading books.
        

---

### **4️⃣ Common Mistakes**

|Mistake|Correct Form|Explanation|
|---|---|---|
|He go to school.|He goes to school.|Add **-s** for third-person singular.|
|She don’t like tea.|She doesn’t like tea.|Use **doesn’t** for third-person singular.|
|Do she play football?|Does she play football?|Use **does** for third-person singular interrogative.|

---

### **5️⃣ Tips for Mastery**

- For **third-person singular**, always check the verb ending carefully.
    
- Avoid adding **-s/-es** in negative and question forms.
    
- Use simple present for **routine and facts**, not for actions happening right now.
