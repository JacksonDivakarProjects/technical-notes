## **Present Perfect Continuous Tense – Comprehensive Guide**

### **1️⃣ Definition**

The **Present Perfect Continuous Tense** expresses:

- Actions that **started in the past and are still continuing**.
    
- Actions that **have been happening recently and are relevant now**.
    
- Emphasis is on **duration** or **continuity** rather than completion.
    

It’s especially useful in **professional settings** for reporting ongoing work, progress, or temporary activities.

---

### **2️⃣ Structure**

**A. Affirmative Sentences**

|Subject|Auxiliary Verb|been|Main Verb + ing|Example|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|I / You / We / They|have|been|working|I have been working on this project for 3 hours.|
|He / She / It|has|been|studying|She has been studying English since morning.|

---

**B. Negative Sentences**

|Subject|Auxiliary + Not|been|Main Verb + ing|Example|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|I / You / We / They|have not (haven’t)|been|working|I haven’t been working on this task.|
|He / She / It|has not (hasn’t)|been|studying|He hasn’t been studying English lately.|

---

**C. Interrogative Sentences**

|Structure|Example|
|---|---|
|Have + subject + been + verb-ing?|Have you been working on this project?|
|Has + subject + been + verb-ing?|Has she been studying English for long?|

---

### **3️⃣ Key Uses with Examples**

1. **Action started in the past and still continuing**
    
    - I have been reading this book for two hours.
        
    - She has been working here since 2018.
        
2. **Recent actions with present relevance**
    
    - He looks tired because he has been running.
        
    - I’m late because I have been waiting for the bus.
        
3. **Temporary actions**
    
    - They have been staying at a hotel while their house is being repaired.
        
4. **Emphasizing duration**
    
    - We have been discussing this project all morning.
        
    - He has been practicing the piano since 5 PM.
        

---

### **4️⃣ Common Mistakes**

|Mistake|Correct Form|Explanation|
|---|---|---|
|I have working here for 2 hours.|I have been working here for 2 hours.|Missing **been**.|
|She has studying since morning.|She has been studying since morning.|Missing **been**.|
|They has been playing since morning.|They have been playing since morning.|Subject-auxiliary agreement.|
|I have been finish the report.|I have been finishing the report.|Main verb must be in **-ing form**.|

---

### **5️⃣ Tips for Mastery**

- Always use **have/has + been + verb-ing**.
    
- Use **for** (duration) or **since** (starting point) to show ongoing time.
    
- Focus is on **duration or ongoing nature**, not completion.
    
- Often used with **look, seem, feel, appear** to describe results of an ongoing action.
