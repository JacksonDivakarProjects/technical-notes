### **Q9: Discuss the Role of ICT (Information and Communication Technology) and E-Governance in Rural Development.**

#### **1. Introduction (2 Marks)**

- **Definition:** ICT in agriculture refers to the use of digital tools (phones, internet, satellites, computers) to improve the livelihood of the rural population.
    
- **E-Governance:** The delivery of government services (subsidies, records, licenses) to citizens electronically.
    
- **Goal:** To bridge the "Digital Divide" (the gap between urban and rural areas) and empower farmers with information.
    

#### **2. Key Areas of Application (6 Marks)**

You should list these specific areas where ICT transforms rural life.

**A. Land Records Digitization (E-Governance)**

- **Old Way:** Farmers had to bribe officials or wait weeks to get a paper copy of their land deed.
    
- **New Way (ICT):** Systems like **Bhoomi (Karnataka)** or **Patta Chitta (Tamil Nadu)** allow farmers to get land records online instantly at a kiosk. This reduces corruption and saves time.
    

**B. Market Information (E-Marketing)**

- **Old Way:** Farmers sold to the first trader they met, unaware of the real price.
    
- **New Way:** SMS services and apps (like **e-NAM**) provide daily prices of commodities in different markets. Farmers can decide _where_ and _when_ to sell.
    

**C. Financial Inclusion**

- **Old Way:** Relying on moneylenders with high interest rates.
    
- **New Way:** **Kisan Credit Cards (KCC)** and mobile banking allow direct transfer of subsidies (DBT - Direct Benefit Transfer) to the farmer's bank account, bypassing middlemen.
    

**D. Agricultural Extension (E-Learning)**

- **Old Way:** Waiting for a government officer to visit the village (which rarely happened).
    
- **New Way:** Video conferences, WhatsApp groups, and call centers (Kisan Call Center - 1551) connect scientists directly to farmers to solve crop problems.
    

#### **3. Benefits of ICT in Rural Development (3 Marks)**

- **Empowerment:** Information is power. A farmer who knows the market price cannot be cheated.
    
- **Speed:** Disaster relief (e.g., cyclone warnings) reaches villages instantly via SMS.
    
- **Transparency:** Digital records reduce the scope for bribery in government offices.
    

#### **4. Challenges (2 Marks)**

- **Digital Literacy:** Many older farmers cannot use smartphones or computers.
    
- **Infrastructure:** Poor internet connectivity and electricity in remote villages.
    
- **Language Barrier:** Most content is in English; it needs to be in local regional languages (Tamil, Hindi, etc.) to be useful.
