### **Q2: Explain the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) phenomenon. Differentiate between El Niño and La Niña and discuss their impacts on global agriculture.**

#### **1. Introduction: What is ENSO? (2 Marks)**

- **Definition:** ENSO (El Niño-Southern Oscillation) is a recurring climate pattern involving changes in the temperature of waters in the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean.
    
- **The "Oscillation":** It is a cycle. The ocean and atmosphere switch back and forth between warming (El Niño) and cooling (La Niña) phases.
    
- **Significance:** It is the single most important driver of year-to-year climate variability, affecting rainfall, temperature, and crop yields globally.
    

#### **2. El Niño ("The Little Boy") – The Warm Phase (4 Marks)**

- **Mechanism:** During normal conditions, trade winds blow west, pushing warm water toward Asia and Australia. During El Niño, these **trade winds weaken** or even reverse.
    
- **Ocean Response:** The pool of warm water flows back toward the east (South America/Peru). This causes Sea Surface Temperatures (SST) in the central/eastern Pacific to rise significantly.
    
- **Atmospheric Response:** The storm clouds move east with the warm water.
    
- **Impact:**
    
    - **South America (Peru/Ecuador):** Causes heavy rains and flooding. It halts the "upwelling" of cold, nutrient-rich water, killing fish stocks.
        
    - **India & Australia:** Causes **suppressed rainfall, hotter temperatures, and severe droughts**. This is disastrous for agriculture in these regions.

#### **3. La Niña ("The Little Girl") – The Cool Phase (4 Marks)**

- **Mechanism:** La Niña is essentially the opposite or "super-normal" version of El Niño. The **trade winds become stronger** than usual, blowing continuously from east to west.
    
- **Ocean Response:** Warm water is pushed even further west toward Asia and Australia. Deep, cold water rises to the surface (upwelling) along the South American coast.
    
- **Impact:**
    
    - **South America:** Drier conditions and cooler temperatures. Excellent for fishing due to nutrient-rich cold water.
        
    - **India & Australia:** Causes **heavier than normal rainfall, monsoons, and floods**. While rain is good, excessive rain can damage standing crops.
        

#### **4. Comparison Table (Crucial for Marks) (2 Marks)**

|**Feature**|**El Niño**|**La Niña**|
|---|---|---|
|**Ocean Temp (Pacific)**|Unusually **Warm**|Unusually **Cool**|
|**Trade Winds**|**Weaker** or Reversed|**Stronger** than normal|
|**Impact on India/Australia**|**Droughts**, Heatwaves, Low Yields|**Floods**, Heavy Monsoons, Bumper Crops|
|**Impact on Americas**|Floods, Heavy Rain|Droughts, Dry Weather|
|**Agricultural Strategy**|Plant drought-resistant crops; Reduce stocking rates.|Prepare drainage systems; Plan for potential waterlogging.|

#### **5. Agricultural Implications (1 Mark)**

Farmers use ENSO forecasts to manage risk.

- **Strategic Decision:** If an El Niño is forecast, an Australian wheat farmer might decide **not to plant** high-yield crops that need lots of water, opting instead for a hardy, low-cost crop to minimize financial loss.
    
- **Tactical Decision:** If La Niña is forecast, farmers might clean out drainage ditches early to handle expected floods.
