### **Question: The "New" Seven Management Tools**

**Importance:**

> *"Be careful! Do not confuse these with the 'Old' 7 QC Tools (Pareto, Histogram, etc.). The 'Old' tools are for **Numerical Data**. The 'New' tools are for **Verbal Data** (Ideas, planning, organizing thoughts). Examiners love to trick students by asking for 'The Seven Management Tools' (New) vs 'The Seven Basic Tools' (Old). If you write the wrong list, you get Zero."*

#### **The Question**

**List the Seven New Management and Planning Tools. Explain their applications and construction with suitable examples.**

---

#### **The Answer**

**1. Introduction**
The "New Seven Tools" (N7) were developed by the Japanese Society for QC (JSQC) to organize verbal information and solve complex planning problems. While the Old 7 tools analyze *data*, the New 7 tools analyze *ideas*.

**2. The 7 New Management Tools**

**1. Affinity Diagram (KJ Method)**

* **Purpose:** To organize a chaotic mess of ideas into natural groups.
* **Process:**
* Brainstorm 100+ ideas on sticky notes.
* Silence! (No talking).
* Move the notes around until they form clusters (groups) that "feel" right.
* Give each cluster a Header title.


* **Application:** Used at the start of a project when you have mixed confusion.
* **Diagram:** Clusters of boxes with headers.

**2. Interrelationship Diagraph (ID) / Relations Diagram**

* **Purpose:** To identify logical causal relationships between different ideas (Cause  Effect).
* **Process:**
* Write the problems/ideas in a circle.
* Draw arrows connecting them (A causes B).
* Count the "In" and "Out" arrows.
* **Root Cause:** The item with the most "Out" arrows.


* **Application:** When the problem is complex and everything seems connected.

**3. Tree Diagram (Systematic Diagram)**

* **Purpose:** To break down a broad goal into specific actionable tasks (Top-down approach).
* **Process:**
* Start with the Main Goal on the left (e.g., "Improve Quality").
* Branch out into "Means" (How?).
* Branch further into specific "Tasks".


* **Application:** Similar to a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) in project management.
* **Diagram:** Looks like a horizontal family tree.

**4. Matrix Diagram**

* **Purpose:** To show the relationship between two or more groups of information.
* **Process:**
* Put Group A on the vertical axis (Row).
* Put Group B on the horizontal axis (Column).
* Mark the intersection with symbols ( Strong,  Medium,  Weak).


* **Application:** QFD (House of Quality) uses this tool heavily. Ideally used to assign tasks (Who does What?).

**5. Matrix Data Analysis Chart**

Link : http://nikunjbhoraniya.com/2020/11/matrix-data-analysis-diagram.html

* **Purpose:** To visualize complex numerical data from a matrix diagram. It is the *only* numerical tool among the New 7.
* **Process:**
* Uses "Principal Component Analysis" (PCA).
* Plots items on an X-Y graph to see clusters.


* **Application:** Market research (e.g., Positioning a car based on "Sporty vs. Luxury" and "Cheap vs. Expensive").

**6. Process Decision Program Chart (PDPC)**

- *Definition : * A Process Decision Program Chart (PDPC) in Total Quality Management (TQM) is ==a visual planning tool to **anticipate potential problems** in a project or process and proactively develop **[contingency plans](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=6922e7dcbe816ab2&q=contingency+plans&source=lnms&fbs=AIIjpHxCtmkhHKu27CW9pNYJlh4RjL2XNN7H2eGEwe1vRehTgx2PNlKqSsUGSlpdjHDY7h78dYWNpI-Nc023idAsZIZE2Om9OclGIrc1yfoMzHNjiXJ1v58lrKS-2bf5wyvRmqRiGEZcOwnFyE8Lm9fsNo0kNYZZzyKpta90ySB_HJf4ikcHlH1DNgq26pzjg_kHFLgpvIPssxeskhIGpgKuLPlORRgxW1jj5OsXgyGYGPOoPVUnA3w&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjysZmGttGRAxWUSWwGHVX-OEkQgK4QegQIARAE&biw=1920&bih=961&dpr=1&mstk=AUtExfALLMFhI3cyo7O5gDypqrxx8DlYlWaJJ0Co8BRilTa_rwAGLJJbXi2sDQEWwZnWvB_Q7UfyI3YfgJ6EWXEcJjBU-UdTKmAltemDo9BXx6eDhwXb47lujAago8TizK-zszEymSYIfTrsdqTTSycv4SDcyiOvUWc-hbCXMkyTFc-kNyEQCC7dtNv3tI_M5epyoYF5&csui=3) (countermeasures)**, ensuring smoother execution, risk mitigation, and goal achievement by systematically asking "what if" questions and preparing responses before issues arise== 

* **Purpose:** To anticipate hidden risks and prepare countermeasures ("Plan B").
* **Process:**
* Draw the ideal plan flow.
* At every step, ask "What could go wrong?"
* Draw a branch for the error.
* Draw a box for the countermeasure.


* **Application:** Risk Management. It prevents surprises during implementation.

**7. Arrow Diagram (Activity Network Diagram)**


- *Definition:* In Total Quality Management (TQM), an **Arrow Diagram** (or Activity Network Diagram) ==visually maps out complex project tasks, showing sequences, dependencies, and durations to find the _critical path_ (longest time-determining route)==
* **Purpose:** To calculate the best schedule and find the "Critical Path" for a project.
* **Process:**
* Draw nodes (circles) and arrows (activities).
* Estimate time for each activity.
* Connect them in sequence.


* **Application:** PERT / CPM (Critical Path Method) in construction or project planning.
