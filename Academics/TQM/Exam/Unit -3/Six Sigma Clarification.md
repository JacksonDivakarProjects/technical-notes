## ✅ Core Idea in One Line

> In Six Sigma, a **safe interval (±6σ) is deliberately chosen**, and **any output falling outside this interval is classified as a defect** because it violates customer specifications.

## ✅ What the “interval” really represents

- The **interval = specification limits**
    
    - **LSL (Lower Specification Limit)**
        
    - **USL (Upper Specification Limit)**
        
- These are **customer-defined tolerance boundaries**, not random statistical lines.
    
- The company **designs the process so that mean ± 6σ lies fully inside these limits**.
    

This guarantees:

- Outputs **inside the interval → acceptable quality**
    
- Outputs **outside the interval → error-prone → counted as defects**
    

---

## ✅ Why this reduces defects in practice

Because:

- The **process spread (σ) is kept very small**
    
- The **allowed tolerance is large in comparison**
    
- So the **probability of crossing the boundary becomes extremely low**
    
- Hence, **defect rate collapses to 3.4 DPMO**
