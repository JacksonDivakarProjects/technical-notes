### **Question 4: Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA)**

**Importance Quote:**

> _"This is the 'Safety Net' question. Engineers love FMEA because it calculates risk numerically. The examiners will ask you to explain the process and often ask you to calculate the Risk Priority Number (RPN). If you remember the formula RPN = S × O × D, you have the key to this answer."_

#### **The Question**

**What is Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA)? Explain the stages of FMEA and the calculation of the Risk Priority Number (RPN) with an example.**

---

#### **The Answer**

1. Definition:

FMEA is a systematic, proactive method for identifying where and how a product or process might fail (Failure Mode) and assessing the relative impact of different failures (Effects), in order to identify the parts of the process that are most in need of change.

**2. Types of FMEA:**

- **Design FMEA (DFMEA):** Focuses on product design (e.g., Will the phone overheat?).
    
- **Process FMEA (PFMEA):** Focuses on the manufacturing process (e.g., Will the soldering machine overheat?).
    

3. Key Metrics (The "S-O-D" Ratings):

Each failure mode is rated on a scale of 1 to 10 for three factors:

- **Severity (S):** How serious is the failure?
    
    - 1 = Not noticeable.
        
    - 10 = Hazardous/Life-threatening (without warning).
        
- **Occurrence (O):** How frequently does this failure happen?
    
    - 1 = Extremely unlikely.
        
    - 10 = Very high/Inevitable failure.
        
- **Detection (D):** If it fails, will we catch it before it reaches the customer?
    
    - 1 = Certain to detect (Good).
        
    - 10 = Impossible to detect (Bad).
        

4. Risk Priority Number (RPN):

This is the most critical calculation in FMEA. It ranks the risks.

$$RPN = \text{Severity (S)} \times \text{Occurrence (O)} \times \text{Detection (D)}$$

- **Rule:** The higher the RPN, the more urgent the problem. You must take action to lower the RPN.
    

**5. The FMEA Procedure (Steps):**

1. **Select the process/component** to analyze.
    
2. **Identify potential Failure Modes:** (How can it break? e.g., "Brake fluid leaks").
    
3. **Identify the Effects:** (What happens? e.g., "Car fails to stop").
    
4. **Identify Causes:** (Why? e.g., "Sealant worn out").
    
5. **Assign Ratings (S, O, D):** Give numbers 1-10.
    
6. **Calculate RPN:** Multiply them.
    
7. **Prioritize:** Focus on the highest RPNs.
    
8. **Take Action:** Redesign or improve controls.
    
9. **Recalculate RPN:** Verify the improvement.
    

**6. Example (Car Brake System):**

- **Failure Mode:** Brake fluid leakage.
    
- **Effect:** Loss of braking power (Very dangerous). $\rightarrow$ **Severity (S) = 10**.
    
- **Cause:** Poor quality rubber seal. $\rightarrow$ **Occurrence (O) = 4** (Occasional).
    
- **Detection:** No sensor to warn the driver. $\rightarrow$ **Detection (D) = 8** (Hard to detect).
    
- Calculation:
    
    $$RPN = 10 \times 4 \times 8 = 320$$
    
- **Conclusion:** An RPN of 320 is very high. Immediate action is needed (e.g., add a dashboard warning light to improve Detection).
    

---

#### **Key Points Summary**

- **Goal:** Prevent failures _before_ they happen.
    
- **Formula:** **RPN = S × O × D**.
    
- **Scales:** 1 (Good/Low risk) to 10 (Bad/High risk).
    
- **Logic:** High RPN = High Priority.
