### **Question 1: Total Productive Maintenance (TPM)**

**Importance Quote:**

> _"This is the 'King' of Unit 4 questions. Anna University evaluators look for the '8 Pillars' diagram. If you know the 8 pillars and the difference between TQM and TPM, you capture 80% of the marks immediately."_

#### **The Question**

**Explain the concept of Total Productive Maintenance (TPM). Discuss its objectives and describe the eight pillars of TPM in detail.**

#### **The Answer**

1. Definition:

TPM is a maintenance program which involves a newly defined concept for maintaining plants and equipment. The goal of the TPM program is to markedly increase production while, at the same time, increasing employee morale and job satisfaction.

- **Core Philosophy:** "Medical science of machines."
    

**2. Objectives of TPM (The "Zero" Concept):**

- Zero Breakdowns.
    
- Zero Accidents.
    
- Zero Defects.
    
- Zero Speed Losses.
    

**3. TQM vs. TPM (Crucial Distinction):**

- **TQM:** Focuses on Output Quality (Software/Service/Product).
    
- **TPM:** Focuses on Equipment Health (Hardware/Machines).
    

**4. The 8 Pillars of TPM (The "Meat" of the answer):**

- **Pillar 1: Jishu Hozen (Autonomous Maintenance):** Operators take care of small maintenance tasks (cleaning, oiling) rather than waiting for maintenance staff. "My Machine, My Responsibility."
    
- **Pillar 2: Kobetsu Kaizen (Focused Improvement):** Small, continuous improvements to eliminate losses.
    
- **Pillar 3: Planned Maintenance:** Scheduled maintenance based on predicted failure rates (Time-based or Condition-based).
    
- **Pillar 4: Quality Maintenance (Hinshitsu Hozen):** Maintaining equipment conditions to ensure zero defects in the final product.
    
- **Pillar 5: Education and Training:** Training operators to upgrade their skills.
    
- **Pillar 6: Office TPM:** Applying TPM principles to administrative and support functions.
    
- **Pillar 7: Safety, Health, and Environment (SHE):** Ensuring a safe working environment.
    
- **Pillar 8: Development Management (Early Equipment Management):** Using lessons from existing machines to design new, maintenance-free equipment.
    

**5. Benefits:**

- Increased productivity (OEE - Overall Equipment Effectiveness).
    
- Reduced customer complaints.
    
- Reduced manufacturing costs.
