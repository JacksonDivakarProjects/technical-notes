### **Question 2: Quality Function Deployment (QFD) / House of Quality**

**Importance Quote:**

> _"This is the single most technical question in Unit 4. If you can draw the 'House of Quality' structure correctly, you secure passing marks instantly. It connects what the customer 'wants' to 'how' we build it. It is a favorite for 13-mark questions because it tests both theory and process."_

#### **The Question**

**Define Quality Function Deployment (QFD). Explain the step-by-step procedure for constructing the 'House of Quality' (HOQ) with a neat diagram.**

#### **The Answer**

1. Definition:

Quality Function Deployment (QFD) is a systematic process for translating "Voice of the Customer" (Subjective needs) into appropriate "Voice of the Engineer" (Objective technical requirements) at every stage of product development.

- **Origin:** Developed by Yoji Akao in Japan (1966).
    

2. The Structure: House of Quality (HOQ)

The primary tool of QFD is the House of Quality matrix. It is called a "House" because the correlation matrix forms a triangular roof.

**3. Steps to Build the House of Quality (The 6 Rooms):**

- **Step 1: Customer Requirements ("The Whats"):**
    
    - List what the customer wants (Voice of Customer).
        
    - _Example:_ For a car, this might be "Easy to open door," "Fast acceleration."
        
- **Step 2: Technical Descriptors ("The Hows"):**
    
    - List technical specifications to meet the "Whats."
        
    - _Example:_ "Door closing energy (Joules)," "Engine Horsepower."
        
- **Step 3: Relationship Matrix (The Center Room):**
    
    - Map the relationship between Whats and Hows.
        
    - Use symbols:
        
        - STRONG relationship (● or 9 points)
            
        - MEDIUM relationship (○ or 3 points)
            
        - WEAK relationship (▽ or 1 point)
            
- **Step 4: Interrelationship Matrix (The Roof):**
    
    - Identifies trade-offs between technical descriptors.
        
    - _Example:_ Increasing "Horsepower" might negatively affect "Fuel Economy." (Conflict).
        
- **Step 5: Customer Competitive Assessment (The Right Wall):**
    
    - Compare your product with competitors based on customer perception (scale 1-5).
        
- **Step 6: Technical Competitive Assessment (The Basement):**
    
    - Prioritize technical targets based on importance ratings and difficulty.
        

4. Phases of QFD:

QFD is not just one house; it is a four-phase process:

1. **Product Planning:** Customer needs $\rightarrow$ Design requirements.
    
2. **Part Deployment:** Design requirements $\rightarrow$ Part characteristics.
    
3. **Process Planning:** Part characteristics $\rightarrow$ Manufacturing operations.
    
4. **Production Planning:** Manufacturing operations $\rightarrow$ Quality control charts.
    

**5. Benefits:**

- Reduces product development time (by 30-50%).
    
- Reduces engineering changes later in the process.
    
- Ensures the final product satisfies the customer.

#### **Key Points Summary (For Quick Revision)**

- **Keyword:** "Voice of Customer" to "Voice of Engineer."
    
- **Core Diagram:** House of Quality (HOQ).
    
- **The Roof:** Shows technical trade-offs/conflicts.
    
- **The Center:** Shows relationship between "Whats" and "Hows."
    
- **Scoring Tip:** Always draw the empty house structure (Left wall, Roof, Right wall, Basement) even if you don't fill in specific data.
