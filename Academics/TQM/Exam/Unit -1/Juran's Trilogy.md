#### **The Answer**

1. Introduction

Dr. Joseph Juran, often called the "Father of Quality Management," taught that managing for quality consists of three basic processes, just like managing for finance. He called this the Juran Trilogy.

He famously stated: "Quality does not happen by accident; it must be planned."

**3. The Three Processes Explained**

Phase 1: Quality Planning (Design the System)

This is the stage where the organization prepares to meet quality goals. It happens before production starts. If planning is poor, the process will begin with a high level of chronic waste.

- **Goal:** To provide the operating forces with the means to produce products that can meet the customer's needs.
    
- **Steps in Planning:**
    
    1. Identify the customers (Internal and External).
        
    2. Determine the needs of those customers.
        
    3. Translate those needs into our language (Technical specs).
        
    4. Develop a product that can respond to those needs.
        
    5. Optimize the product features to meet our needs and customer needs.
        
    6. Develop a process that is able to produce the product.
        
    7. Transfer the process to the operating forces (Operations).
        

Phase 2: Quality Control (Maintain the Standard)

This occurs during actual operations. The goal is to keep the process stable and prevent it from getting worse.

- **The "Sporadic Spike":** In the diagram, you see a sudden spike in defects. This is a special cause (e.g., a power failure or a broken tool).
    
- **Role of Control:** The operating team acts like firefighters. They detect the spike, find the cause, and fix it to return the process to its original "Zone of Quality Control."
    
- **Steps in Control:**
    
    1. Evaluate actual performance.
        
    2. Compare actual performance to quality goals.
        
    3. Act on the difference (Gap analysis).
        

Phase 3: Quality Improvement (Breakthrough)

This is where the magic happens. Even if the process is "under control," it might still be generating a high level of waste (e.g., 10% scrap rate). This waste is "Chronic" (it's always there).

- **Chronic Waste:** This is the cost of doing business _unless_ the system is changed.
    
- **Breakthrough:** Management must step in to change the system fundamentally to lower the chronic waste to a new, lower level.
    
- **Steps in Improvement:**
    
    1. Prove the need for improvement (Show the cost of poor quality).
        
    2. Identify specific projects for improvement.
        
    3. Organize teams to guide the projects.
        
    4. Diagnose the causes (Root Cause Analysis).
        
    5. Provide a remedy and prove that the remedy works.
        
    6. Deal with resistance to change.
        
    7. Control the new level of performance (Hold the gains).
        

4. Juran’s 10 Steps to Quality Improvement

To make the answer "content rich," list Juran's specific roadmap for Phase 3:

1. Build awareness of the need and opportunity for improvement.
    
2. Set goals for improvement.
    
3. Organize to reach the goals (establish a quality council).
    
4. Provide training.
    
5. Carry out projects to solve problems.
    
6. Report progress.
    
7. Give recognition.
    
8. Communicate results.
    
9. Keep score.
    
10. Maintain momentum by making annual improvement part of the regular systems and processes of the company.
    

11. Conclusion

The Juran Trilogy teaches that "Control" is not enough. Control only maintains the status quo. To survive, companies must engage in "Planning" to prevent defects and "Improvement" to reduce chronic waste.

### **Suggestions to Improve Marks**

1. **The Graph Labels:** Be very precise with the labels on the graph.
    
    - Label the "Sporadic Spike" as **"Uncontrolled Variation."**
        
    - Label the drop in the curve as **"Quality Improvement (Breakthrough)."**
        
    - Label the shaded area at the bottom as **"Chronic Waste"** or **"Cost of Poor Quality."**
        
2. **Financial Analogy:** Explicitly write this comparison table to show deep understanding:
    
    - **Quality Planning** = Budgeting.
        
    - **Quality Control** = Cost Control / Expense Monitoring.
        
    - **Quality Improvement** = Cost Reduction / Profit Improvement.
        
3. **Terminology:** Use the word **"Chronic"** versus **"Sporadic"**. These are Juran's trademark terms. Examiners look for them.
