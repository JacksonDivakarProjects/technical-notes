### **Question 1: The Role of Senior Management & The Quality Council**

**Importance:**

> _"Leadership is the 'Driver' of TQM. Without it, the car doesn't move. This question often appears as: 'Explain the role of senior management' or 'Discuss the structure and functions of a Quality Council'. It is a straightforward theory question that requires you to list specific duties."_

#### **The Question**

**Explain the roles of Senior Management in TQM implementation. Describe the structure and duties of the Quality Council.**

#### **The Answer**

1. Introduction

TQM begins at the top. The most common reason for TQM failure is the lack of commitment from senior management. Senior managers must move from being "Bosses" (who command) to "Leaders" (who coach and facilitate).

**2. Roles of Senior Management (The TQM Leaders):**

- **Establish the Vision:** Define where the company is going (e.g., "To be the world's best phone manufacturer").
    
- **Create the Culture:** actively promote a culture of trust, openness, and continuous improvement. They must "Walk the Talk."
    
- **Allocate Resources:** Provide the necessary money, time, and training for quality improvement projects.
    
- **Remove Barriers:** Identify and remove obstacles that stop employees from doing good work (e.g., outdated machines, bad policies).
    
- **Reward and Recognize:** Personally recognize employees who achieve quality goals to show that quality matters.
    

3. The Quality Council (The Steering Committee)

To drive TQM, senior management usually forms a team called the Quality Council. This is the "Supreme Court" of quality in the company.

**A. Structure (Who is in it?):**

- **Chairperson:** CEO or General Manager.
    
- **Members:** Heads of major departments (Production, HR, Sales, Design, Finance).
    
- **Coordinator:** The Quality Manager (acts as the secretary).
    

**B. Duties of the Quality Council:**

- **Develop the Quality Policy:** Write the core statement of what the company believes in.
    
- **Establish Strategic Goals:** Set the long-term quality targets (e.g., "Reduce defects by 50% in 2 years").
    
- **Select Projects:** Choose which specific problems (e.g., "Fix the leaking paint machine") the teams should work on.
    
- **Monitor Progress:** Meet monthly to review the status of improvement teams.
    
- **Provide Resources:** Ensure teams have the tools and time they need.
    

4. Characteristics of Successful Quality Leaders:

(Add this to make the answer "content rich")

1. **They give priority to the Customer:** The customer comes first, profit comes second.
    
2. **They empower subordinates:** They trust their workers to make decisions.
    
3. **They emphasize improvement:** They are never satisfied with the status quo.
    
4. **They encourage collaboration:** They break down silos between departments.
    

5. Conclusion

Senior management cannot delegate responsibility for quality. As Dr. Deming said, "Quality is made in the boardroom." The Quality Council is the vehicle they use to drive this responsibility throughout the organization.
