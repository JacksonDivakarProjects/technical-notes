#### **The Question**

**Define Customer Satisfaction. Explain the Kano Model of customer satisfaction in detail. What are the various methods used to collect customer feedback?**

---

#### **The Answer**

1. Introduction to Customer Satisfaction

Customer satisfaction is the state where the customer feels that the product or service has met or exceeded their expectations. In TQM, the "Customer" is not just the person who buys the product (External Customer), but also the next person in the production line (Internal Customer).

- The Equation:
    
    $$\text{Satisfaction} = \text{Perception} - \text{Expectation}$$
    
    - If Perception > Expectation $\rightarrow$ Delighted.
        
    - If Perception = Expectation $\rightarrow$ Satisfied.
        
    - If Perception < Expectation $\rightarrow$ Dissatisfied.
        

2. The Teboul Model (The Triangle of Satisfaction)

To understand satisfaction, we must balance three factors:

1. **Company Needs:** (Profit, survival).
    
2. **Customer Needs:** (Quality, price, delivery).
    
3. Product/Service: (The meeting point).
    
    Satisfaction occurs only at the intersection of these three.
    

4. The Kano Model (CRITICAL for 13 Marks)

Dr. Noriaki Kano developed a model that classifies customer preferences into three categories. Not all "good" features create satisfaction in the same way.Shutterstock

_(Imagine a graph: X-axis is "Functionality", Y-axis is "Satisfaction")_

- **1. Basic Needs (Must-be Qualities):**
    
    - **Definition:** These are requirements that the customer assumes will be there. If they are present, the customer is **neutral**. If they are missing, the customer is **extremely dissatisfied**.
        
    - **Example:** Clean sheets in a hotel, or brakes in a car. You don't thank the hotel for clean sheets, but you complain loudly if they are dirty.
        
    - _Motto:_ "You get no credit for these, only blame."
        
- **2. Performance Needs (One-dimensional Qualities):**
    
    - **Definition:** These result in satisfaction when fulfilled and dissatisfaction when not fulfilled. The relationship is linear.
        
    - **Example:** Battery life in a phone. The longer it lasts, the happier you are. The shorter it lasts, the unhappier you are.
        
    - _Motto:_ "More is better."
        
- **3. Excitement Needs (Delighters):**
    
    - **Definition:** These are unexpected features. If they are present, the customer is **delighted**. If they are missing, the customer is **not dissatisfied** (because they didn't expect them).
        
    - **Example:** Getting a free chocolate on your hotel pillow, or a car that parks itself.
        
    - _Motto:_ "Surprise and Delight."
        

4. Methods of Collecting Customer Feedback (Listening to the Voice of Customer)

To satisfy customers, you must listen to them. Here are the standard content-rich tools:

- **1. Comment Cards:** Simple cards attached to the product or left at the counter. (Low cost, but low response rate).
    
- **2. Surveys / Questionnaires:** Structured questions sent via email or mail. Good for getting data on specific "Performance Needs."
    
- **3. Focus Groups:** Bringing 6-10 customers together with a moderator to discuss the product in depth. Good for identifying "Excitement Needs."
    
- **4. Toll-Free Phone Lines:** 1-800 numbers for complaints. This provides immediate feedback on defects.
    
- **5. Customer Visits:** Managers visiting the customer’s site to see how the product is actually being used.
    
- **6. Ghost Shopping:** Hiring a mystery shopper to pretend to be a customer and rate the service (common in hotels/retail).
    

5. Customer Retention (Why it matters)

Satisfaction is useless if the customer doesn't come back.

- **The Cost Rule:** It costs **5 to 10 times more** to acquire a new customer than to retain an existing one.
    
- **The Ripple Effect:** A happy customer tells 1 person; an unhappy customer tells 10 people (especially on social media).
    
- **Strategy:** Shift focus from "Marketing" (hunting new customers) to "Relationship Management" (farming existing customers).
    

6. Conclusion

Customer satisfaction is a moving target. What is an "Exciter" today (e.g., WiFi in a hotel in 2005) becomes a "Basic Need" tomorrow (WiFi in a hotel today). TQM requires constantly using the Kano Model to innovate and stay ahead of expectations.
