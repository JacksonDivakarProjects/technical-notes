### **Question 3: Employee Motivation (Maslow & Herzberg)**

**Importance:**

> _"TQM is not just about machines; it is about people. You cannot achieve Quality if your employees are unhappy. This question is a staple in Unit 2. #### **The Question**

**Define Motivation. Explain Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs and Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory of motivation. How do these theories apply to TQM?**

---

#### **The Answer**

1. Introduction

Motivation is the process of stimulating people to action to accomplish desired goals. In TQM, a motivated workforce is essential because quality comes from the pride employees take in their work. A worker who feels safe, respected, and valued will produce fewer defects than one who is fearful or ignored.

2. Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs

Dr. Abraham Maslow proposed that human needs are arranged in a hierarchy (a pyramid). A person cannot focus on higher-level needs until the lower-level needs are satisfied.Shutterstock

- **Level 1: Physiological Needs (Survival)**
    
    - **Definition:** These are the basic biological needs for survival: food, water, shelter, and sleep.
        
    - **Workplace Application:** A fair base salary, a safe working environment (AC/Heating), and a cafeteria. If a worker is starving, they don't care about "Quality."
        
- **Level 2: Safety Needs (Security)**
    
    - **Definition:** Protection from physical and emotional harm. The need for stability and freedom from fear.
        
    - **Workplace Application:** Job security (no layoffs), pension plans, insurance, and safe machinery (OSHA standards).
        
- **Level 3: Social Needs (Belonging)**
    
    - **Definition:** The need for affection, belonging, acceptance, and friendship. Humans are social animals.
        
    - **Workplace Application:** Friendly supervision, teamwork, office parties, and a "family" atmosphere. TQM Quality Circles fulfill this need perfectly.
        
- **Level 4: Esteem Needs (Ego)**
    
    - **Definition:** The need for self-respect, autonomy, status, recognition, and attention.
        
    - **Workplace Application:** Job titles, awards ("Employee of the Month"), recognition programs, and promotions.
        
- **Level 5: Self-Actualization (Fulfillment)**
    
    - **Definition:** The drive to become what one is capable of becoming. It is the need for growth and achieving one's potential.
        
    - **Workplace Application:** Challenging work, opportunities for innovation, and allowing employees to solve complex problems (Empowerment).
        

3. Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory

Frederick Herzberg modified Maslow’s theory specifically for the workplace. He found that the factors that make people happy are different from the factors that make them unhappy.Shutterstock

**A. Hygiene Factors (Maintenance Factors):**

- **Definition:** These factors do **NOT** motivate employees to work harder. However, if they are _missing_, employees become dissatisfied.
    
- **The "Kick":** They only prevent dissatisfaction; they don't create satisfaction.
    
- **Examples:**
    
    - Company Policy & Administration.
        
    - Supervision (Relationship with Boss).
        
    - Salary (Money is often just a hygiene factor!).
        
    - Working Conditions.
        
    - Job Security.
        

**B. Motivators (Satisfiers):**

- **Definition:** These are the factors that actually push people to achieve higher performance and quality. They deal with the _content_ of the job itself.
    
- **Examples:**
    
    - Achievement (Completing a tough task).
        
    - Recognition (Being noticed for good work).
        
    - The Work Itself (Is it interesting?).
        
    - Responsibility (Being trusted).
        
    - Advancement (Growth).
        

**4. Comparison & TQM Application**

- **The Trap:** Many managers try to motivate people by just increasing salary or fixing the lighting (Hygiene). This only results in "Neutral" employees, not "Motivated" ones.
    
- **The TQM Solution:** To get true Quality, you must use **Motivators**.
    
    - _Empowerment_ gives Responsibility.
        
    - _Continuous Improvement_ gives Achievement.
        
    - _Quality Awards_ give Recognition.
        

5. Conclusion

Maslow describes the needs of the human, while Herzberg describes the incentives of the job. For a TQM program to succeed, the company must first ensure Hygiene factors are met (fair pay, safety) so employees aren't angry, and then focus heavily on Motivators (empowerment, recognition) to drive excellence.
