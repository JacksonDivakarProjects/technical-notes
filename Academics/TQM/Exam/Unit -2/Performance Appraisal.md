### **Question 4: Performance Appraisal**

**Importance:**

> _"This is a double-edged sword in TQM. While it is a standard HR process, TQM gurus (like Deming) actually criticized traditional appraisals. To score 13 marks, you must explain the standard process AND the modern '360-degree' method, which is more aligned with TQM principles. Examiners look for the specific methods (Rating Scales, 360-degree) and the step-by-step procedure."_

#### **The Question**

**What is Performance Appraisal? Explain the objectives and the detailed process of performance appraisal. Discuss the different methods used.**

---

#### **The Answer**

1. Introduction

Performance Appraisal is the systematic evaluation of the performance of employees and to understand the abilities of a person for further growth and development. It is a periodic review (usually annual) where a manager assesses an employee's work against a set of targets or standards.

**2. Objectives of Performance Appraisal**

- **Compensation:** To decide on salary increments, bonuses, and rewards.
    
- **Promotion:** To identify who is ready for a higher position.
    
- **Training Needs:** To find out the skill gaps where training is required.
    
- **Feedback:** To tell the employee where they stand and how they can improve.
    
- **Motivation:** To recognize good work and boost morale (if done correctly).
    

3. The Performance Appraisal Process

This is a cycle that repeats every year. You should list these steps clearly:

1. **Establish Performance Standards:**
    
    - Define what "Good Performance" looks like. (e.g., "Sales of ₹10 Lakhs" or "0 Defects").
        
    - Standards must be clear, objective, and measurable (SMART goals).
        
2. **Communicate Standards:**
    
    - The manager must explain the goals to the employee _at the start_ of the year. It should not be a surprise.
        
3. **Measure Actual Performance:**
    
    - Collect data throughout the year. Use reports, observation, and feedback.
        
4. **Compare Actual vs. Standards:**
    
    - Measure the gap. Did they meet, exceed, or fail the target?
        
    - _Note:_ Consider external factors (e.g., "Sales were low because of Covid," not because the employee was lazy).
        
5. **Discuss the Appraisal (The Interview):**
    
    - The manager and employee meet. This is the most critical step. It should be a constructive dialogue, not an interrogation.
        
    - Focus on the _problem_, not the _person_.
        
6. **Take Corrective Action / Decision:**
    
    - **Positive:** Promotion, Reward, Coaching for next level.
        
    - **Negative:** Warning, Training, Demotion, or Dismissal.
        

7. Methods of Performance Appraisal

To make the answer "Content Rich," categorize the methods into Traditional and Modern.

**A. Traditional Methods (Past-Oriented):**

- **1. Ranking Method:** The manager ranks employees from Best to Worst (1st to last). _Drawback:_ It creates bitter competition.
    
- **2. Graphic Rating Scale:** The most common method. A form with traits (Reliability, Teamwork, Output) rated on a scale of 1-5 (Poor to Excellent). _Drawback:_ Subjective (Halo Effect).
    
- **3. Critical Incident Method:** The manager records specific "good" or "bad" events that happened during the year (e.g., "Handled a fire emergency well").
    
- **4. Checklist:** A Yes/No list of traits (e.g., "Is he regular?" Yes/No).
    

**B. Modern Methods (Future-Oriented & TQM Friendly):**

- **1. 360-Degree Appraisal:**
    
    - **Definition:** Feedback is collected from everyone around the employee: The Boss, Subordinates, Peers (Colleagues), and even Customers.
        
    - **Benefit:** It removes bias. If the Boss hates you but everyone else loves you, the score balances out. It gives a complete picture.
        
- **2. Management by Objectives (MBO):**
    
    - **Definition:** The employee and manager _jointly_ set the goals. At the end of the year, they evaluate performance based _only_ on those goals.
        
    - **Benefit:** High commitment because the employee helped set the target.
        

**5. Deming’s Criticism (The TQM Twist)**

- Important to mention: Dr. Deming (Point 12) argued that traditional appraisals **destroy teamwork**. They encourage employees to fight for a higher ranking rather than helping each other.
    
- **TQM Approach:** Focus on Team Appraisal rather than just Individual Appraisal.
    

6. Conclusion

An effective appraisal system is not just about judging the past; it is about improving the future. In TQM, the appraisal should focus on development and coaching rather than just punishment or pay raises.
