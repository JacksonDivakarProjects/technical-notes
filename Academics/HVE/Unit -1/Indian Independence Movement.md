### **Question:**

**Trace the timeline and key events of the Indian Independence Movement from 1757 to 1947. Discuss the role of major movements and leaders in achieving freedom.**

---

### **Answer Structure:**

#### **1. Introduction**

The Indian Independence Movement was a series of historic events with the ultimate aim of ending British rule in India. It spanned from the initial resistance in 1757 to the final victory in 1947. The struggle evolved from regional rebellions to a mass nationalist movement led by the Indian National Congress (INC) and Mahatma Gandhi.

#### **2. The Onset of British Rule and Early Resistance (1757 - 1857)**

- **Battle of Plassey (1757):**
    
    - This battle marked the beginning of the British East India Company's political control over India.
        
    - _Context:_ The British defeated the Nawab of Bengal, Siraj-ud-Daulah, establishing the foundation of colonial rule.
        
- **The Revolt of 1857 (Sepoy Mutiny):**
    
    - Often called the "First War of Independence."
        
    - **The Cause:** The introduction of the Enfield rifle. Soldiers (Sepoys) believed the cartridges were greased with cow and pig fat, which offended both Hindu and Muslim religious sentiments (as cows are sacred to Hindus and pigs are forbidden to Muslims).
        
    - **The Spark:** Soldiers refused to bite the cartridges and killed British officers.
        
    - **Key Leaders:**
        
        - **Rani Lakshmibai (Queen of Jhansi):** Fought bravely against the British annexation policy.
            
        - **Tantia Tope:** A brilliant military strategist and close associate of Nana Saheb and the Rani of Jhansi.
            
    - **Result (1858):** The revolt was suppressed (Gwalior fell, Rani Lakshmibai was killed). However, it ended the East India Company's rule, and India came under the direct control of the British Crown (Queen Victoria).
        

#### **3. Rise of Nationalism and The Partition of Bengal (1885 - 1911)**

- **Formation of INC (1885):**
    
    - The Indian National Congress was founded on December 28, 1885, by **A.O. Hume** with 72 delegates in Mumbai. It initially aimed to seek more rights for Indians under British rule.
        
- **Partition of Bengal (1905):**
    
    - Implemented by **Lord Curzon**.
        
    - **Divide and Rule:** The province was divided into East Bengal (Muslim majority) and West Bengal (Hindu majority) to weaken Hindu-Muslim unity.
        
    - **Reaction:** It sparked the **Swadeshi Movement** (boycott of British goods).
        
    - **Reunification (1911):** Due to massive protests, the British were forced to annul the partition in 1911.
        

#### **4. World War I and The Gandhian Era (1914 - 1919)**

- **First World War (1914-1918):**
    
    - Indians were forced to participate in the war to support Britain.
        
    - **Impact:** The war drained India's economy, caused inflation, and was followed by a deadly Influenza epidemic.
        
- **Champaran Satyagraha (1917):**
    
    - Gandhiji’s first major act of civil disobedience in India.
        
    - **Issue:** Farmers in Bihar were forced to grow **Indigo** (blue dye) instead of food crops and were exploited.
        
    - **Method:** Gandhi used non-violent protest (Satyagraha) and successfully negotiated relief for the farmers.
        
- **Rowlatt Act & Jallianwala Bagh (1919):**
    
    - **Rowlatt Act (Black Act):** Gave the government power to imprison any person without trial (No appeal, no lawyer).
        
    - **Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (April 13, 1919):** In Punjab, General Dyer opened fire on a peaceful unarmed gathering, killing hundreds. This tragedy united the nation in anger and paved the way for mass movements.
        

#### **5. Mass Movements for Freedom (1920 - 1942)**

- **Non-Cooperation Movement (1920):**
    
    - Launched by Gandhi to protest the Jallianwala Bagh massacre.
        
    - Indians surrendered titles, boycotted schools, courts, and foreign clothes.
        
    - _Note:_ The movement was suspended in **1922** after the **Chauri Chaura incident** (where a mob turned violent), leading to Gandhi’s imprisonment for 2 years.
        
- **Civil Disobedience Movement & Dandi March (1930):**
    
    - **Salt March (Dandi Yatra):** The British had a monopoly on salt production and levied a tax on it.
        
    - Gandhi walked from Sabarmati to Dandi to break the salt law by making his own salt. It symbolized that Indians would no longer bow to unfair British laws.
        
- **Quit India Movement (1942):**
    
    - Launched during World War II with the slogan **"Do or Die"**.
        
    - It was a demand for an immediate end to British rule. Most leaders were arrested immediately, but the masses continued the protest.
        

#### **6. The Final Phase and Independence (1947)**

- **Post-War Scenario:** The British economy was shattered after WWII, and they could no longer hold onto India.
    
- **Partition and Independence:**
    
    - Based on the **Mountbatten Plan**, British India was partitioned on religious lines.
        
    - **August 14, 1947:** Formation of Pakistan.
        
    - **August 15, 1947:** India gained Independence.
        
    - Jawaharlal Nehru became the first Prime Minister, delivering the famous "Tryst with Destiny" speech.
        

#### **Conclusion**

The Indian Independence movement was not just a political struggle but a social evolution. It moved from the violent uprising of 1857 to the non-violent mass movements of Mahatma Gandhi. The unity of diverse religions and regions against colonial rule eventually forced the British to leave, birthing the world's largest democracy.
