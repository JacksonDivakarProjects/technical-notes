### **Priority Question 1 (Unit 4 - Social Ethics)**

**Q: Discuss the role of Constitutional Protections in promoting gender equality. How do specific Articles and Acts address social discrimination in India?**

---

### **13-Mark Answer Structure**

**1. Introduction**
* **Definition:** Gender equality is not just a fundamental human right but a necessary foundation for a peaceful, prosperous, and sustainable world.
* **Context:** The Constitution of India serves as the bedrock for gender justice. The Preamble itself sets the tone by securing "Justice" (Social, Economic, and Political) and "Equality" (of status and opportunity) for all citizens.
* **Thesis:** The Constitution not only prohibits discrimination but also empowers the State to take affirmative action to uplift women and marginalized genders.

**2. Key Constitutional Provisions (The "Magna Carta" of Rights)**
*You must quote these Articles to score high marks.*

* **Article 14 (Equality before Law):**
    * Ensures that no person is above the law. Men and women have equal legal standing.
* **Article 15 (Prohibition of Discrimination):**
    * **15(1):** The State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
    * **15(3) [Crucial Exception]:** This allows the State to make **"special provisions"** for women and children. This is the legal basis for affirmative action (e.g., reserved seats for women in local bodies or special scholarships).
* **Article 16 (Equality of Opportunity in Employment):**
    * Guarantees equal chances for all citizens in matters of public employment, preventing gender bias in hiring for government jobs.
* **Article 21 (Protection of Life and Personal Liberty):**
    * Interpreted by the Supreme Court (e.g., in the *Vishaka* Guidelines) to include the **right to live with human dignity**, free from sexual harassment and violence.
* **Article 39(d) (Directive Principle):**
    * Mandates "Equal Pay for Equal Work" for both men and women.
* **Article 51A(e) (Fundamental Duties):**
    * It is the duty of every citizen to **renounce practices derogatory to the dignity of women.**

**3. Legislative Acts Supporting the Constitution**
*While the Constitution gives the vision, Acts provide the teeth.*

* **POSH Act (2013):** (Prevention of Sexual Harassment) mandates Internal Complaints Committees (ICC) in workplaces.
* **Domestic Violence Act (2005):** Protects women from physical, emotional, and economic abuse at home.
* **The Equal Remuneration Act (1976):** Prevents wage discrimination based on sex.



**4. Role of These Protections in Addressing Discrimination**
* **Legal Deterrence:** Strong laws create a fear of penalty for discriminatory practices (e.g., dowry, harassment).
* **Economic Empowerment:** By mandating equal pay (Art 39d) and maternity benefits (Art 42), the law ensures women can remain in the workforce.
* **Political Representation:** The 73rd and 74th Amendments (mandating 33% reservation in Panchayats) have brought over a million women into grassroots governance.
* **Social Change:** Laws like the prohibition of Child Marriage challenge deep-rooted patriarchal norms.

**5. Diagram Recommendation**
* *Is a diagram needed?* **Yes, highly recommended for a 13-marker.**
* **Draw this:** A central circle labeled **"Gender Justice"**. Draw 5 spokes radiating out, each labeled with an Article (Art 14, Art 15, Art 21, Art 39, Art 51A). This provides a quick visual summary for the examiner.

**6. Conclusion**
The Indian Constitution acts as a powerful tool for social engineering. By combining **Negative Rights** (prohibiting discrimination) with **Positive Rights** (enabling affirmative action), it aims to dismantle historical social discrimination. However, constitutional equality must be translated into social reality through education and strict implementation.
