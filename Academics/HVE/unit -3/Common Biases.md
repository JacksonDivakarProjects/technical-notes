Here are the **most common types of biases**, explained in **simple, practical language** with real-life relevance. These are exactly what show up in **exams, interviews, analytics, and decision-making at work**.

---

## ✅ **1. Confirmation Bias**

**What it is:**
You only accept information that supports what you already believe.

**Example:**
You think a tool is bad → you only notice negative reviews.

**Impact:**
Kills objective thinking. Very dangerous in **data analysis and research**.

---

## ✅ **2. Selection Bias**

**What it is:**
Your sample is **not representative** of the full group.

**Example:**
Surveying only gym users to judge national fitness levels.

**Impact:**
Leads to **false conclusions**.

---

## ✅ **3. Sampling Bias**

**What it is:**
Some people have **no chance of being selected**.

**Example:**
Online survey about poverty → poor people may not have internet.

---

## ✅ **4. Cognitive Bias**

**What it is:**
Mental shortcuts that distort logical thinking.

**Example:**
Judging someone as smart just because they speak confidently.

---

## ✅ **5. Anchoring Bias**

**What it is:**
You rely too heavily on the **first number you hear**.

**Example:**
First price shown is ₹50,000 → ₹40,000 feels cheap even if it’s expensive.

---

## ✅ **6. Observer Bias**

**What it is:**
Your expectations affect what you observe.

**Example:**
A teacher expecting a student to fail may judge them more harshly.

---

## ✅ **7. Survivorship Bias**

**What it is:**
You only look at **success stories** and ignore failures.

**Example:**
“Everyone becomes rich from startups” — ignoring failed startups.

---

## ✅ **8. Reporting Bias**

**What it is:**
People **hide or change** the truth in reports.

**Example:**
Employees don’t admit mistakes in surveys.

---

## ✅ **9. Halo Effect**

**What it is:**
One good trait makes you assume everything about a person is good.

**Example:**
Good looks → assumed to be intelligent.

---

## ✅ **10. Gender / Social Bias**

**What it is:**
Judging people based on **gender, caste, age, or background**.

**Impact:**
Leads to **unfair decisions and hiring errors**.

---

## ✅ **Power One-Line Exam Answer**

“Bias is a systematic error in thinking, sampling, or observation that leads to incorrect conclusions.”
