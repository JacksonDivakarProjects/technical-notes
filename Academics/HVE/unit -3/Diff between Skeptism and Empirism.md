This is a great specific question. These two concepts are often confused, but they play very different roles in the scientific method.

**Empiricism** is the **Source** of knowledge (Data), while **Skepticism** is the **Filter** of knowledge (Doubt).

Here is the difference broken down for your exam.

### **1. Core Definitions**

* **Empiricism (The "Evidence" Approach):**
    * The philosophy that all knowledge comes from **sensory experience** and **observation**.
    * *Motto:* "Don't just tell me, show me the data."
    * *Role in Science:* It drives the collection of facts through experiments and measurements.

* **Skepticism (The "Questioning" Approach):**
    * The philosophy of **doubt**. It is the suspension of belief until there is sufficient evidence to prove a claim.
    * *Motto:* "Are you sure? Is there another explanation?"
    * *Role in Science:* It drives the critical analysis of the facts to ensure they aren't fake, biased, or coincidental.

---

### **2. Key Differences (Comparison Table)**

| Feature | Empiricism (The Engine) | Skepticism (The Brakes) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Focuses on **gathering data** through senses/instruments. | Focuses on **questioning the validity** of that data. |
| **Action** | Observation, Experimentation, Measurement. | Critical Thinking, Peer Review, Re-testing. |
| **Goal** | To build a body of factual evidence. | To eliminate errors, lies, and bias. |
| **Key Question** | "What did we observe?" | "Is the observation accurate?" |
| **Example** | Measuring that a patient's fever dropped after taking a pill. | Asking if the fever dropped because of the pill or just naturally. |

---

### **3. How They Work Together (The Scientific Cycle)**

Science cannot exist without both.
1.  **Empiricism** provides the raw material (The Evidence).
2.  **Skepticism** processes that material to ensure it is high quality (The Verification).

**Example:**
* **Empiricism:** "I saw a white crow." (Observation)
* **Skepticism:** "Are you sure it wasn't a seagull? Let's catch it and check its DNA." (Verification)
* **Result:** Valid Scientific Knowledge.
