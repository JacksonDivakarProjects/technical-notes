### **8. The "Sources of Error" List (Use for Limitation/Design Questions)**

If a question asks about **"Factors affecting performance," "Accuracy,"** or **"Why robots fail,"** use these 3 universal technical terms:

- **Backlash:** The mechanical "play" or gap between gears. It causes the robot to lose accuracy when changing direction.
    
- **Hysteresis:** The difference in output when approaching a point from two different directions (e.g., moving up to 10mm vs. moving down to 10mm).
    
- **External Noise:** Electrical interference (from welding arcs or heavy motors) that corrupts sensor signals.
    

---

### **9. The "Mechatronics Architecture" (Hardware Filler)**

If you are asked to **"Explain the System"** for _any_ robot, sensor, or drive, and you don't know the specific parts, break it down into these 4 subsystems:

1. **Power Unit:** Provides energy (Electricity/Air/Oil) to the system.
    
2. **Actuator Unit:** Converts energy into motion (The "Muscle").
    
3. **Sensor Unit:** Monitors the environment (The "Eyes").
    
4. **Control Unit:** Processes data and issues commands (The "Brain").
    

> _Write a sentence for each. It fills half a page easily._

---

### **10. The "Industry 4.0" Buzzwords (Use for Conclusion/Future Scope)**

To make your Unit 5 answers look modern and high-tech, end with a paragraph about **Current Trends**. Examiners love these keywords:

- **IoT (Internet of Things):** The robot is connected to the internet to share data.
    
- **Digital Twin:** A virtual 3D replica of the robot used for simulation before the real robot moves.
    
- **Cobots (Collaborative Robots):** Robots designed to work safely _alongside_ humans without fences (e.g., Padded joints, force sensors).
    
- **Predictive Maintenance:** Using AI to predict when a motor will fail _before_ it breaks.
    

---

### **11. The "Economic Analysis" Points (Use for 'Justification' Questions)**

If asked **"Why should a company buy a robot?"** (Economic feasibility), use these standard business terms:

- **Payback Period:** How long it takes for the robot's savings to cover its purchase cost (usually 2-3 years).
    
- **ROI (Return on Investment):** The profit generated by the robot over its lifetime.
    
- **Cycle Time Reduction:** Robots work faster than humans, increasing units produced per hour.
    
- **Scrap Reduction:** Robots make fewer mistakes, wasting less raw material.

### **12. The "Universal Diagram 2": The Signal Flow**

This is different from the Feedback Loop. Use this for **Sensors, Vision, or ADC/DAC questions**.

**Draw this chain:**

`Physical Variable (Heat/Light)` $\rightarrow$ `Transducer` $\rightarrow$ `Signal Conditioning (Amplifier/Filter)` $\rightarrow$ `ADC` $\rightarrow$ `Microcontroller`

- **Explanation:** "The raw physical signal is weak and noisy. It must be amplified and filtered (Signal Conditioning) before being converted to digital data for the processor."
    

---

### **13. The "Standard Safety Procedure" (Unit 5 Filler)**

If asked about **"Safe Operation"** or **"Maintenance"**, list these steps:

1. **Risk Assessment:** Identify potential hazards before starting.
    
2. **Lockout/Tagout (LOTO):** Physically locking the power switch so no one can turn it on during maintenance.
    
3. **Slow Speed Testing (T1 Mode):** Running the program at 10% speed to check for collisions.
    
4. **Emergency Stop Verification:** Testing the Red Button before every shift.
