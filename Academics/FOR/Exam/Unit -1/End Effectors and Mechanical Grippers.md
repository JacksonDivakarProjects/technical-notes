# End Effectors and Mechanical Grippers

### 1. Definition

An **End Effector** is a device attached to the end of a robot's wrist that allows the robot to perform a specific task. It is often referred to as the "Hand" of the robot.

---

### 2. Classification of End Effectors

End effectors are broadly divided into two categories:

1. **Grippers:** Used to grasp and hold objects (e.g., picking up a part).
    
2. **Tools:** Used to perform a process (e.g., a welding torch or a spray paint nozzle).
    

---

### 3. Types of Mechanical Grippers

Mechanical grippers use movable "fingers" to grasp objects. They are classified based on their movement mechanism:

#### **A. Two-Finger Grippers**

- **Pivoting (Angular) Gripper:** The fingers rotate around a pivot point like a pair of scissors.
    
- **Translating (Parallel) Gripper:** The fingers move in a straight line toward each other, remaining parallel at all times. This is better for objects with flat surfaces.
    

#### **B. Vacuum (Suction) Grippers**

- **Mechanism:** Uses suction cups and a vacuum pump to hold parts.
    
- **Advantage:** Excellent for picking up flat, smooth items like glass sheets or cardboard boxes.
    
- **Constraint:** Does not work on porous (holed) or very rough surfaces.
    

#### **C. Magnetic Grippers**

- **Mechanism:** Uses permanent magnets or electromagnets to pick up objects.
    
- **Application:** Best for handling metallic (ferromagnetic) parts like steel plates or scrap metal.
    
- **Advantage:** Very fast and requires only one surface to be accessible.
    

#### **D. Adhesive Grippers**

- **Mechanism:** Uses a sticky substance or surface tension to hold the object.
    
- **Application:** Used for light-weight, fragile materials like fabrics or paper.
    

---

### 4. Factors in Gripper Selection (Marks Booster)

To score extra marks, list the criteria used to choose a gripper:

- **Weight of the object:** Determines the required gripping force.
    
- **Shape and Geometry:** Round objects need different fingers than flat objects.
    
- **Surface Condition:** Fragile surfaces need "soft" or "vacuum" grippers to avoid damage.
    
- **Operating Environment:** High heat or chemicals may require specific materials.
    

---

### 5. Summary Table for Quick Comparison

|**Gripper Type**|**Best For**|**Main Advantage**|
|---|---|---|
|**Mechanical**|Solid, rigid parts|Strong and versatile|
|**Vacuum**|Flat, smooth sheets|Simple and light|
|**Magnetic**|Iron and Steel parts|Fast pickup|
