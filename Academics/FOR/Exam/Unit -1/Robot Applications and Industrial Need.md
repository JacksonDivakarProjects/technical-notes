# Unit 1: Robot Applications and Industrial Need

### 1. The Need for Robots (Part B - 13 Marks)

In the exam, this is often asked as: *"Why do we need robots in industry? Discuss the advantages and disadvantages."*

#### **A. Reasons for Using Robots (The "3Ds")**

Industries typically implement robots for tasks that fall under the **3Ds**:

* **Dirty:** Tasks like waste management or mining where human hygiene is at risk.
* **Dangerous:** Environments involving high radiation, toxic chemicals, or extreme heat (e.g., foundry work).
* **Dull:** Highly repetitive tasks that lead to human fatigue and errors, such as assembly line sorting.

#### **B. Advantages of Robots**

* **Consistency:** Robots perform the same task with identical precision every time, ensuring high product quality.
* **Increased Productivity:** They can work 24/7 without breaks, significantly increasing output.
* **Safety:** Robots take over hazardous roles, reducing workplace accidents and insurance costs.
* **Precision:** Capable of handling miniature components (like microchips) that are too small for human hands.

#### **C. Disadvantages of Robots**

* **High Initial Cost:** The expense of purchasing and installing a robotic system is very high.
* **Job Displacement:** Automation can lead to the loss of unskilled manual labor jobs.
* **Maintenance:** Requires highly skilled engineers to program and fix the robots when they fail.

### 2. Different Applications of Robots (High-Score Elaborated)

You should categorize these to show breadth of knowledge.

#### **Category 1: Material Handling**

* **Pick and Place:** Moving parts from a conveyor belt to a storage bin.
* **Palletizing:** Stacking heavy boxes or bags onto pallets for shipping.

#### **Category 2: Processing Operations**

* **Spot Welding:** Specifically used in the automotive industry to join car body panels.
* **Spray Painting:** Provides a uniform coating while keeping humans away from toxic fumes.
* **Arc Welding:** Used for continuous metal joining in shipbuilding or construction.

#### **Category 3: Assembly and Inspection**

* **Electronic Assembly:** Robots like the SCARA are used to insert tiny components into PCBs.
* **Inspection:** Robots equipped with machine vision check for defects in parts at very high speeds.

### 3. Robot Parts and Their Functions (Part A - 2 Marks / Part B - 13 Marks)

To "elaborate" on this, you must describe the **Manipulator**, **Controller**, and **Sensors** as a single system.

| Component | Function | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Manipulator** | The Body | The physical structure of links and joints that moves in space. |
| **Controller** | The Brain | The computer system that stores programs and controls movements. |
| **End Effector** | The Hand | The tool or gripper at the end of the arm that performs the task. |
| **Actuators** | The Muscle | Devices (motors/cylinders) that provide energy for movement. |
| **Sensors** | The Senses | Devices that provide feedback about the robot's position and environment. |
