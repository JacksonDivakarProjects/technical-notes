### **Question: What are End Effectors? Classify them. Explain the working principle of Mechanical, Vacuum, and Magnetic Grippers with neat sketches.**

#### **1. Introduction**

An End Effector is the device attached to the robot's wrist that interacts with the environment. It is the "hand" of the robot.

They are broadly classified into two categories:

1. **Grippers:** Used to grasp and hold objects (e.g., for Pick and Place).
    
2. **Tools:** Used to perform an operation (e.g., Welding gun, Spray painting gun, Grinder).
    

---

#### **2. Mechanical Grippers (The "Fingers")**

These are the most common type. They use mechanical fingers to grasp an object.

- **Working Principle:** They normally use a **Pneumatic Cylinder** or Electric Motor to drive a mechanism that opens/closes the fingers.
    
- **Mechanisms:**
    
    - **Pivot/Linkage Type:** Like scissors. Good for cylindrical parts.
        
    - **Rack and Pinion:** The fingers move in parallel. Good for flat or square parts.
        
- **Diagram Tip:** Draw a simple "Scissor" mechanism connected to a piston.
    

---

#### **3. Vacuum Grippers (Suction Cups)**

Used for handling flat, smooth, and fragile objects like glass sheets, metal plates, or cardboard boxes.

- **Construction:** Consists of **Suction Cups** made of rubber or silicone.
    
- **Working Principle (Venturi Effect):**
    
    1. Compressed air flows through a **Venturi nozzle**.
        
    2. This creates a low-pressure zone (vacuum) inside the cup.
        
    3. Atmospheric pressure pushes the object against the cup.
        
- **Advantages:** Gentle handling (won't crush the part), cheap, lightweight.
    
- **Disadvantages:** Requires a clean, flat surface.
    

---

#### **4. Magnetic Grippers**

Used strictly for **Ferrous materials** (Iron, Steel).

- **Types:**
    
    1. **Permanent Magnets:** Always "ON." Requires a "Stripper" mechanism (a mechanical pin) to push the part off to release it.
        
    2. **Electromagnets:** "ON" when current flows, "OFF" when current stops. Easier to control but drops the part if power fails.
        
- **Advantages:** Can pick up parts with holes or irregular shapes; very fast pickup (no finger movement needed).
    
- **Disadvantages:** Only works on magnetic metals; picks up metal dust/chips (needs cleaning).
    

---

#### **5. Selection Criteria (How to choose?)**

If you need to fill space, list these factors for selecting a gripper:

- **Part Properties:** Weight, material, surface finish.
    
- **Environment:** Is it hot? (Rubber cups might melt). Is it dusty? (Magnets attract dust).
    
- **Production:** Cycle time required.
