### **Answer (13-Mark Level)**

The final phase of a project involves refining the written document and defending the work verbally. Unit 5 emphasizes accuracy and communication skills.

#### **1. Proofreading and Formatting (The "Polish")**

Before submission, the report must undergo rigorous checking to ensure professional quality.

- **Avoiding Typographical Errors:** Simple spelling or grammar mistakes undermine credibility. You must check for "Typos" (typing errors) and grammatical consistency3.
    
    - _Tip:_ Do not rely solely on spell-checkers; they miss context errors (e.g., "trail" vs. "trial").
        
- **Bibliography in Required Format:** Citations must follow a standard style (e.g., APA or IEEE) consistently. This acknowledges the sources used in the Literature Review (Unit 4) and avoids plagiarism4.
    
- **Font and Spacing:** The visual layout must be uniform. Standard academic formatting usually requires:
    
    - **Font:** Times New Roman or Arial (Size 12 for text, 14/16 for headings).
        
    - **Spacing:** 1.5 or Double line spacing for readability5.
        
- **Checking Tables and Illustrations:** Ensure every table and graph is clear, labeled correctly, and referenced in the text. A table without a number or caption is incomplete6.
    

#### **2. Techniques for Oral Presentation (The "Defense")**

The oral presentation is where you "sell" your project to the examiners.

- **Visual Aids:** Use slides to highlight key points, not to read from. Slides should contain diagrams, charts, and bullet points—never long paragraphs.
    
- **Delivery Techniques:**
    
    - **Voice:** Speak clearly and at a moderate pace. Modulate your tone to emphasize important findings.
        
    - **Eye Contact:** Engage the audience (examiners). Do not stare at the screen or your notes.
        
    - **Body Language:** Stand confident. Avoid fidgeting or crossing arms.
        
- **Handling the Q&A:** This is often the most critical part. Listen to the question fully before answering. If you don't know an answer, admit it honestly or explain how you would find out, rather than guessing.
