## Recommended Paper Structure (IEEE)

### **I. Introduction**

- Motivation and real-world context
    
- Problem statement
    
- Research gap and contribution
    
- Objectives of the study
    

---

### **II. Literature Review**

Use **L2 subsections** (as discussed earlier):

- **A. Vision-Based Footfall Analytics**
    
- **B. Confidence and Uncertainty in Computer Vision**
    
- **C. Analytics Systems and Decision Support**
    
- **D. Decision Stability and Operational Implications**
    
- **E. Research Gap and Positioning**
    

---

### **III. Methodology**

(Use “Methodology” or “Proposed Framework” — both are IEEE-acceptable)

Suggested L2 subsections:

- **A. System Overview**
    
- **B. Data Acquisition and Preprocessing**
    
- **C. Temporal Aggregation Strategy**
    
- **D. Baseline Footfall Analytics**
    
- **E. Confidence-Aware Aggregation Framework**
    
- **F. Decision Rule Definition**
    
- **G. Evaluation Metrics (Flip Rate, Stability)**
    

This section is where your **formulas and calculations** live.

---

### **IV. Experimental Setup**

Keeps methods and experiments cleanly separated (reviewers prefer this).

- **A. Dataset Description**
    
- **B. Parameter Settings**
    
- **C. Experimental Protocol**
    
- **D. Implementation Details**
    

---

### **V. Results**

Purely factual. No interpretation yet.

- **A. Signal-Level Comparison**
    
- **B. Decision-Level Comparison**
    
- **C. Trend Analysis**
    

Tables and figures appear here.

---

### **VI. Discussion**

Critical interpretation.

- **A. Impact of Confidence Weighting**
    
- **B. Analysis of Decision Stability**
    
- **C. Boundary Conditions and Negative Findings**
    
- **D. Practical Implications**
    

This is where you defend why _no change_ is still meaningful.

---

### **VII. Limitations and Future Work**

Short but important.

- Data constraints
    
- Temporal assumptions
    
- Extension to multi-day or live deployments
    

---

### **VIII. Conclusion**

- Summary of findings
    
- Key contribution
    
- Final take-away
    

---

### **References**

IEEE numbered style.

## Optional (only if required by journal)

## What NOT to add

- “System Architecture” as a standalone section
    
- “Results and Discussion” combined (weakens clarity)
    
- “Case Study” (unless explicitly required)
